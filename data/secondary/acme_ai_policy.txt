Acme Corp Responsible AI and Data Use Policy (Sample Corporate Document)

Purpose and Scope
This policy governs how employees of Acme Corp may use artificial intelligence tools, including large language models and generative AI services, in the course of their work. It applies to all employees, contractors, and interns. The goal is to capture the productivity benefits of AI while protecting customer data, intellectual property, and the company's legal and ethical obligations. This is a sample document used to demonstrate that the retrieval system works on ordinary business content, not only on security material.

Approved Tools
Employees may use AI tools that have been reviewed and approved by the Information Security and Legal teams. The current approved list is maintained on the internal portal. Tools that have an enterprise agreement with data protection terms are preferred, because consumer versions of AI tools may use submitted data to train their models.

Prohibited Data
Employees must never paste the following categories of information into any external AI tool that is not on the enterprise-approved list: customer personal data, payment card information, unreleased financial results, trade secrets, source code marked confidential, or anything covered by an NDA. When in doubt, employees should treat data as prohibited and ask the Legal team.

Disclosure and Transparency
Any content that is substantially generated by AI and shared externally, such as marketing copy or customer communications, must be reviewed by a human before publication. Employees are accountable for the accuracy of AI-assisted work they submit. The company believes in transparency: customers have the right to know when they are interacting with an automated system rather than a human.

Human Oversight
AI tools may assist with decisions but must not be the sole basis for decisions that significantly affect individuals, such as hiring, termination, credit, or disciplinary action. A qualified human must review and take responsibility for such decisions. This reflects the principle of meaningful human oversight that appears in emerging AI regulations.

Bias and Fairness
Teams deploying AI systems that affect customers or employees must assess those systems for bias against protected groups before launch and periodically thereafter. Documentation of these assessments must be retained. If a system is found to produce materially unfair outcomes, it must be paused until remediated.

Intellectual Property
Work products created with AI assistance may have uncertain copyright status. Employees should consult Legal before relying on AI-generated material for anything that the company intends to protect as proprietary. Employees must also respect the intellectual property of others and not use AI tools to reproduce copyrighted material.

Security of AI Integrations
Any AI feature integrated into a company product must follow the secure development lifecycle. API keys for AI services must be stored in the company secrets manager, never in source code or client-side code. AI features that accept user input must guard against prompt injection, where a malicious user embeds instructions in their input to manipulate the model. Inputs and outputs of AI systems handling sensitive data must be logged for audit.

Reporting Concerns
Employees who observe misuse of AI tools or potential policy violations should report them to their manager or the Ethics hotline. Reports made in good faith are protected from retaliation.
